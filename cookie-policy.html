Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2026

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them on Dev Card Showcase, and what choices you have regarding them. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" (remaining on your device until you delete them) or "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser).

2. How We Use Cookies

Dev Card Showcase uses cookies and similar tracking technologies for the following purposes:

Type of Cookie Purpose Duration
Essential Cookies These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. Session or up to 1 year
Preference Cookies These cookies enable a website to remember information that changes the way the website behaves or looks, like your preferred language or the region you are in. Up to 1 year
Functionality Cookies These cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as your theme preference - light or dark mode) and provide enhanced, more personal features. Persistent (stored locally)
Analytics Cookies These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. Up to 2 years

3. Types of Data Storage We Use

In addition to traditional cookies, we use other types of local storage:

  • Local Storage: We use your browser's local storage to save your theme preference (Light/Dark mode) and any customized settings. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.
  • Session Storage: We may use session storage to temporarily store information about your current visit, such as form data or search preferences.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some third-party services that appear on our website may use their own cookies. These include:

  • GitHub: When you interact with GitHub links or widgets on our site, GitHub may set cookies according to their own policies.
  • Analytics Services: We may use services like Google Analytics to understand website traffic and usage patterns. These services set their own cookies.
  • Social Media Platforms: Links to social media platforms (Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.) may result in those platforms setting cookies when you visit them.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options to control cookies:

Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites or all sites
  • Set preferences for different types of cookies

Opting Out of Analytics

If we use analytics services, you can typically opt-out by:

  • Using the browser controls mentioned above
  • Visiting the analytics provider's opt-out page (if available)
  • Using browser extensions designed to block tracking

Specific Opt-Out on Our Site

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner where you can choose which types of cookies to accept or reject.

6. What Happens If You Disable Cookies?

If you choose to disable cookies, some features of Dev Card Showcase may not function properly:

  • Your theme preference (Light/Dark mode) may not be saved
  • Certain interactive features may not work correctly
  • You may need to re-enter preferences each time you visit

7. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.

8. Contact Us

If you have any questions about our use of cookies or this Cookie Policy, please contact us by opening an issue on our GitHub Repository.
