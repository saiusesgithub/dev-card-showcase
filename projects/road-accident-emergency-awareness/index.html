Road Accident & Emergency Response Guide

Essential information to help you respond safely and effectively during road accidents and emergency situations

IN CASE OF EMERGENCY, CALL 112 (INDIA) OR 911 (INTERNATIONAL) IMMEDIATELY

Immediate Steps After an Accident

  1. 1
    Ensure Safety First

    Check if you and others are safe. Move to a safe location if possible, away from traffic.

  2. 2
    Check for Injuries

    Assess yourself and others for injuries. Do not move seriously injured people unless there's immediate danger.

  3. 3
    Call Emergency Services

    Dial emergency number (112 in India) immediately. Provide clear location and details about injuries.

  4. 4
    Secure the Accident Scene

    Turn on hazard lights, set up warning triangles or flares to alert other drivers.

  5. 5
    Exchange Information

    Share contact and insurance details with other involved parties. Take photos of the scene if safe to do so.

  6. 6
    Report to Authorities

    File an official police report. This is essential for insurance claims and legal purposes.

Do's and Don'ts at Accident Scenes

Do's

  • Stay Calm

    Keep a clear head to assess the situation properly.

  • Provide First Aid

    Assist injured people with basic first aid if trained.

  • Gather Evidence

    Take photos and notes for insurance and legal purposes.

  • Cooperate with Authorities

    Provide accurate information to police and emergency responders.

Don'ts

  • Don't Admit Fault

    Let authorities determine responsibility based on evidence.

  • Don't Move Seriously Injured

    Unless in immediate danger, wait for professional medical help.

  • Don't Leave the Scene

    Leaving an accident scene can have legal consequences.

  • Don't Argue or Blame

    Emotions run high, but focus on safety and cooperation.

Basic First-Aid Awareness

Bleeding Control

Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth. Elevate the wound if possible. Do not remove impaled objects.

CPR

If someone is unresponsive and not breathing, begin chest compressions (100-120 per minute) until help arrives.

Fractures

Immobilize the injured area. Do not try to realign bones. Use splints or supportive materials if available.

Head Injuries

Keep the person still. Do not move unless absolutely necessary. Monitor for consciousness changes.

Burns

Cool burns with running water for 10-20 minutes. Do not use ice. Cover with a clean, non-stick dressing.

Shock Management

Keep the person lying down, elevate legs if no spinal injury suspected. Keep warm and monitor breathing.
