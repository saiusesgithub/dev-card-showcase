Discovery & History

Hydrogen was discovered by Henry Cavendish in 1766. The name comes from the Greek words 'hydro' and 'genes' meaning 'water-forming'. It is the most abundant element in the universe, making up about 75% of all normal matter.

Uses & Applications

Hydrogen is used in ammonia production, petroleum refining, metal treatment, and as a rocket fuel. It has potential as a clean energy carrier in fuel cells. Liquid hydrogen is used in cryogenics and superconductivity studies.
